# LinkedIn Post Writer

Turn any topic into a professional, insight-driven LinkedIn post. Research first, write second — always.

## Step 1: Research the Topic

Run **2–3 targeted WebSearch queries** before writing anything — no more. Aim each query at a distinct angle:
- Recent developments, stats, or news about the topic
- Expert perspectives or industry consensus
- A surprising angle, counterintuitive fact, or underappreciated insight

Work from the search result snippets. Only `WebFetch` a full page when a snippet is genuinely insufficient to ground a claim — full pages are the biggest token cost and are rarely needed for a short post.

You're not summarizing search results — you're mining them for one sharp insight that will make the post worth reading.

## Step 2: Craft the Post

Structure the post in this exact order, with a blank line between every section:

**Hook** — One punchy sentence. Make it specific, provocative, or counterintuitive. Avoid generic openers like "Excited to share..." or "I've been thinking about...". The hook is the reason someone stops scrolling.

**Body** — 3–4 sentences that expand on the hook. Ground it in what you found in your research. Be direct and confident. No fluff, no hedging, no "it's important to note that".

**Bullets** — 3–5 takeaways or supporting points. Always use a hyphen ( - ) at the start of each bullet, never numbers. Keep each bullet to one punchy line.

**Closing line** — One sentence that lands the point or invites reflection. Can be a question, a challenge, or a reframe. Not a sales pitch.

**Hashtags** — 2–3 relevant hashtags on their own line. No more. Choose ones that are genuinely used in the professional community, not generic filler.

## Formatting Rules

- Blank line between every section (hook, body, bullets, closing, hashtags)
- Hyphens for bullets — never numbers, never asterisks, never dots
- No bold or markdown formatting in the post body — LinkedIn renders it inconsistently
- Keep the whole post under 1,300 characters if possible (LinkedIn sweet spot)
- Write in first person, active voice

## Tone

Professional but human. Confident but not arrogant. Insightful, not preachy. The reader should feel like they learned something — not like they were sold to or lectured at.

## Example Structure

```
[Hook sentence that stops the scroll]

[Sentence expanding on the hook.]
[Sentence grounding it in data or reality.]
[Sentence that sharpens the point.]

- [Bullet takeaway one]
- [Bullet takeaway two]
- [Bullet takeaway three]

[Closing line that lands the idea or sparks reflection]

#Hashtag1 #Hashtag2 #Hashtag3
```

After writing the post, confirm the research source(s) you used with a one-line note so the user knows the post is grounded in current information.
